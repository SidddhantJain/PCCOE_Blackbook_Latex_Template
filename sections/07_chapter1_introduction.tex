\chapter{Introduction}

\section{Background}
Elections are a critical democratic process, but traditional and many electronic voting systems still face challenges in security, auditability, and accessibility. Existing systems often focus on vote collection and counting while giving limited attention to transparent audit trails, tamper awareness, inclusive voter journeys, and deployment in constrained environments.

The project \textbf{VoteGuard Pro} addresses this by combining a guided electronic voting workflow with encrypted vote persistence, a verifiable hash-chained audit path, optional biometric analytics, and a blockchain-ready anchoring pathway. The system is designed as a prototype for research and engineering evaluation in academic and controlled environments.

\section{Motivation}
The key motivation is to design a system that treats security and accessibility as equal constraints rather than trade-offs. The project is driven by practical concerns such as the risk of vote tampering and weak post-election verification in opaque systems, a voter trust deficit caused by limited transparency in vote handling, limited support for accessible and guided digital voting experiences, and the need for a modular and testable architecture that can evolve toward blockchain-backed immutability.

\section{Aims and Objectives}
The project aims to build a secure prototype that stores ballots in encrypted append-only form while maintaining an audit chain. It also seeks to implement a guided voter journey that moves from identifier entry to biometric capture, ballot selection, confirmation, and persistence. In addition, the system is designed to prevent duplicate casting within the session, preserve modular boundaries between the UI, storage, security, devices, and optional blockchain adapters, and evaluate the prototype for functionality, usability, accessibility, and operational robustness.

\section{Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) Addressed}
The project aligns with SDG 9 by proposing resilient digital civic infrastructure for elections, with SDG 10 by improving accessibility and guided flows for diverse voter groups, with SDG 16 by improving transparency, auditability, and trust in electoral mechanisms, and with SDG 17 by enabling integration of government, academic, and technical stakeholders.
